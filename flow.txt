You will freeze these models and use them purely as feature extractors.1. The Text Engine (Semantic Context)Target: SamLowe/roberta-base-go_emotions-onnxWhy: The ONNX quantization ensures ultra-low latency. It extracts 28 fine-grained emotional labels (critical for distinguishing grief from sadness).2. The Speech Engine (Acoustic Prosody)Target: superb/hubert-base-superb-erWhy: HuBERT outperforms standard wav2vec models in noisy, real-world voice call environments.3. The Video Engine (Visual Valence)Target (v1 Launch): DeepFace (Simplest to get the pipeline running).Target (v2 Upgrade): AffectNet-trained ResNet-50 (via the Facial-Expression-Recognition-Zoo repository). Provides continuous valence/arousal to detect masked depression (fake smiling).4. The STT Engine (Transcription)Target: Deepgram SDK (API Key required).Why: The fastest streaming speech-to-text available to power the text engine during voice calls.PART 2: THE MASTER ARCHITECTURE FLOWHere is exactly how a piece of user data travels through Veliora, from the moment they speak to the moment the AI responds.Layer 0: Continuous Ingestion & The Audio ForkThe Mechanism: When a user starts a voice call, the system does not wait for them to finish. It captures the continuous WebRTC stream and forks it.Path A (Semantic): The stream flows instantly to Deepgram. Deepgram returns the transcribed text.Path B (Acoustic): Simultaneously, the server buffers the exact same audio stream into short chunks (e.g., 4-second rolling windows).Video: The client captures 1 webcam frame every 2 seconds and sends it to the backend.Layer 1: Parallel Feature ExtractionText Processing: The new Deepgram transcript is appended to Veliora's 5-turn sliding context window. The RoBERTa model processes the last 5 turns to understand the escalation of the conversation, outputting an emotion score.Speech Processing: The audio chunk is passed to HuBERT, which ignores the words and extracts the prosody (pitch, voice breaks, speech rate), outputting an acoustic emotion score.Video Processing: The webcam frame is passed to the vision model to extract the visual Valence (pleasantness) and Arousal (intensity).Layer 1.5: Adaptive Temporal Alignment (The Sync)The Mechanism: Deepgram text and HuBERT audio chunks will arrive at slightly different times due to latency.The Logic: Instead of dropping unmatched signals, the server maintains a sliding temporal buffer. It aligns the RoBERTa text score with the HuBERT acoustic score representing the exact same moment in time, interpolating any minor microsecond gaps.Layer 2: The Fusion Enginev1 (Dynamic Confidence Gating): The system ensembles the Text, Speech, and Video scores. However, if the Speech model's confidence drops (e.g., due to background traffic noise), the system automatically lowers the audio weight and relies 80% on the Text.v2 (Cross-Modal Transformer): Once you have 1,000+ sessions of data, you replace the ensemble with a custom PyTorch Transformer. It fuses the raw mathematical vectors before classification, allowing it to understand that "I am fine" (Text) + A shaky voice (Audio) = Distress.Layer 3: Temporal Trajectory TrackingThe Mechanism: A single sad message means nothing. The system tracks the user's Valence over the last 15 minutes using an Exponential Moving Average (EMA).The Output: It calculates the "Slope." If the slope drops violently, it flags a Volatile Crash. If it drops slowly but stays down, it flags Sustained Distress.Layer 4: The Personalization EngineThe Mechanism: The system pulls the user's 30-day historical baseline from the database.The Logic: It calculates the $\Delta$ (Delta). If a naturally monotone user sounds flat, it is ignored. If a highly energetic user suddenly sounds flat, the Delta is massive, signaling hidden distress.Layer 5: Probabilistic Risk & The Dual-Alert Safety GateThe Mechanism: The system evaluates the Fused Emotion, the Trajectory, and the User Delta to make a safety decision.Tier 1 (Acute Crisis - 0 Day Alert): If the ensemble detects extreme Fear, Grief, a Volatile Crash, or self-harm keywords, the generative AI is instantly bypassed. The system delivers a deterministic emergency script (e.g., iCall/Vandrevala foundation) and locks intervention until the user clicks an acknowledgment button.Tier 2 (Chronic Distress - 10 Day Alert): If the user's rolling Valence average stays below a healthy threshold for 10 consecutive days, the system triggers a gentle, proactive conversational check-in ("I've noticed you've been down lately...").Layer 6: Dynamic LLM Empathy SynthesisThe Mechanism: If no crisis is detected, the full psychological profile (Dominant Emotion, User Delta, Trajectory) is silently injected into the AI persona's system prompt.The Output: Veliora dynamically adjusts its conversational pacing, vocabulary, and empathy level to match the user's exact psychological state without ever breaking character.Layer 7: The Clinical DashboardThe Mechanism: Raw message counts are ignored. The database logs the temporal vectors to power the UI.The Output: The dashboard displays the "Dominant Mood" of the day, a 14-day Valence trajectory line chart, and a critical [Masking Flag] if the video feed (smiling) constantly contradicts the vocal tone (distressed).THE EXECUTION STRATEGYYou do not build this all at once. You build it in phases:Phase 1 (The Plumbing): Build the Layer 0 Audio Fork and get the frozen RoBERTa and HuBERT models to output numbers in your terminal.Phase 2 (The Logic): Build the Temporal Sync (Layer 1.5) and the Dual-Alert Safety Gate (Layer 5).Phase 3 (The Flywheel): Launch. Let users interact. Gather the data.Phase 4 (The Research Vanguard): Use your proprietary data to train the Cross-Modal Transformer for Layer 2.
